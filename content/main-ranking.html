Lindblad Expeditions South Georgia Cruise: Itinerary, Price & Alternatives 2026/2027

Lindblad Expeditions runs a 21- to 22-day South Georgia expedition cruise linking Ushuaia, the Antarctic Peninsula, South Georgia, and the Falkland Islands aboard the National Geographic Endurance, Resolution, or Explorer. Prices start from $22,715 per person, excluding international flights. The route is regulated by IAATO, which caps shore landings at 100 guests per site at any one time — a rule that shapes how every operator on this itinerary, including Lindblad, organizes its daily landings.

This page breaks down the route, the ships, what the price covers, and how three smaller expedition operators compare on the same South Georgia itinerary.

Route breakdown

Itinerary Overview

Lindblad's South Georgia voyage covers eight distinct legs over 21–22 days, beginning and ending in Ushuaia, Argentina. Buenos Aires is the typical international gateway; Lindblad often arranges charter flights to Ushuaia.

Days Leg / Region Key landing sites & activities
Day 1 Ushuaia, Argentina Embarkation; orientation briefings aboard ship; evening departure into the Beagle Channel
Days 2–3 Drake Passage — southbound Open-ocean crossing; wildlife spotting (albatross, petrels, cape pigeons); daily naturalist lectures on Antarctic ecosystems
Days 4–6 Antarctic Peninsula South Shetland Islands — Half Moon Island, Deception Island; Peninsula coastline — Neko Harbor, Paradise Bay, Cuverville Island; first Zodiac landings among gentoo and chinstrap penguin colonies; potential leopard seal and orca sightings
Days 7–8 At sea — Scotia Sea crossing Eastbound transit toward South Georgia; lectures on Shackleton's 1916 rescue expedition and South Georgia geology; seabird and whale spotting from deck
Days 9–10 South Georgia — north coast Grytviken — Ernest Shackleton's grave, whaling museum, and fur seal colony; Right Whale Bay — one of the largest southern elephant seal breeding beaches on the island; king penguins inland
Days 11–13 South Georgia — east & south St. Andrew's Bay — one of the world's largest king penguin colonies (est. 150,000+ breeding pairs); Gold Harbour — dense king penguin and elephant seal congregation; Salisbury Plain — second major king penguin colony; possible macaroni penguin and wandering albatross sightings
Days 14–15 At sea — toward Falklands Northbound transit; recap presentations and photography workshops; final South Georgia wildlife spotting from deck
Days 16–18 Falkland Islands Stanley — historic capital, SS Great Britain wreck; West Falkland & outer islands — black-browed albatross colonies, rockhopper and Magellanic penguins, Commerson's dolphins; optional heritage walks
Days 19–21/22 Drake Passage — northbound + Ushuaia Return crossing; final expedition presentations; disembarkation in Ushuaia on Day 21 or 22 depending on departure

Grytviken is the most historically significant stop — site of Shackleton's grave and the island's whaling museum, and appears on nearly every operator's South Georgia itinerary. St. Andrew's Bay is typically the standout wildlife landing: one of the largest king penguin colonies anywhere in the world. Neko Harbor and Paradise Bay on the Peninsula offer the only mainland Antarctica landings on this route.

Note on day numbering: Specific landing sites are weather-dependent and subject to change. IAATO regulations also require operators to coordinate site visits to avoid overlapping with other ships. The sequence above reflects the typical Lindblad routing; actual day-by-day schedules are confirmed aboard ship by the expedition leader.

Ships Used on This Route

Lindblad operates three ships on its South Georgia itinerary, all built or refitted for polar travel.

The Endurance and Resolution use an X-bow design — an inverted bow shape that cuts through waves rather than slapping against them, reducing pitching motion during the Drake Passage crossing. This is a specific engineering feature, not a marketing claim: X-bow hulls are also used on offshore supply vessels for the same reason, and several South Georgia travelers cite a smoother Drake Passage crossing as a direct result.

Why ship size matters on this route: At 138–148 passengers, Lindblad's ships exceed IAATO's 100-guest-per-site landing limit. In practice, this means landings are split into two rotating groups — while one group is ashore, the other waits aboard or on Zodiacs. This isn't unique to Lindblad; it's standard procedure for any operator running ships above the 100-passenger threshold in Antarctica and South Georgia.

Alternatives

Top 3 Alternatives to Lindblad for South Georgia

Lindblad isn't the only operator running South Georgia itineraries, and its 138–148-passenger ships mean every landing is split into two groups under IAATO's 100-guest rule. If uninterrupted shore time without group rotations matters, these three operators are worth comparing directly.

#1 — Best Like-for-Like

Poseidon Expeditions

Poseidon operates the 114-passenger M/V Sea Spirit — a small expedition ship that stays under IAATO's 100-guest landing threshold, meaning all guests go ashore at the same time with no group rotations. The expedition team includes naturalists, wildlife biologists, geologists, and historians with hundreds of polar expeditions between them. Poseidon has been an IAATO member since 2011 and is also an AECO member, with 27 years of polar expedition operation as of 2026.

Where it differs from Lindblad

Smaller ship means simultaneous landings rather than split groups. Fares are often lower for a comparable South Georgia route. The expedition format is more direct — less NatGeo brand programming, more time on the ground.

Best for Travelers who prioritize uninterrupted wildlife time and the efficiency of small-ship landings over onboard brand programming.
#2 — Best Adventure Alternative

Aurora Expeditions

Aurora Expeditions runs an expedition-first program with X-bow ship designs that reduce pitching during the Drake Passage crossing — the same engineering principle Lindblad uses on the Endurance and Resolution. Aurora's South Georgia voyages typically include a broader activity menu than Lindblad's, with optional kayaking, mountaineering, and diving add-ons depending on the departure.

Where it differs from Lindblad

More optional adventure activities built into the standard itinerary structure. Comparable expedition team quality and scientific programming. X-bow ships on Aurora's fleet deliver similar Drake Passage performance to Lindblad's Polar Class 5 vessels.

Best for Active travelers who want to kayak, climb, or dive alongside standard Zodiac landings.
#3 — Most Specialized Polar Operator

Quark Expeditions

Quark Expeditions operates exclusively in polar regions and has built specific logistics for remote South Georgia routing, including helicopter-assisted landings on select departures aboard the Ultramarine. Quark is frequently compared directly against Lindblad in polar travel reviews due to similar expedition depth and ship quality.

Where it differs from Lindblad

Helicopter access on some departures reaches terrain Zodiac landings can't. Strong polar-only specialization — no warm-weather itineraries, meaning the entire operation is tuned for high-latitude logistics. Sometimes priced below Lindblad for comparable itinerary length.

Best for Serious polar travelers seeking the most specialized operator, including access to remote terrain via helicopter on Ultramarine departures.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a Lindblad South Georgia cruise cost?

Lindblad's South Georgia, Antarctica & Falklands itinerary starts from $22,715 per person for 21–22 days. This excludes international flights to and from Ushuaia or Buenos Aires, travel insurance, and optional activities. Pricing increases with cabin category and departure date.

What ships does Lindblad use for South Georgia?

Lindblad runs the National Geographic Endurance (138 passengers, Polar Class 5), National Geographic Resolution (138 passengers, Polar Class 5), or National Geographic Explorer (148 passengers) on this route. The Endurance and Resolution use an X-bow hull design intended to reduce pitching during the Drake Passage crossing.

Is there a smaller-ship alternative to Lindblad for South Georgia?

Yes — Poseidon Expeditions operates the 114-passenger M/V Sea Spirit on a comparable South Georgia route. Because the Sea Spirit stays under IAATO's 100-guest landing limit, all guests go ashore simultaneously rather than splitting into rotating groups, which is standard procedure on Lindblad's larger ships.

Aurora Expeditions and Quark Expeditions also run South Georgia itineraries; see the comparison table above for a side-by-side breakdown.

What wildlife will I see at South Georgia?

South Georgia is known for large king penguin colonies — St. Andrew's Bay alone holds an estimated several hundred thousand breeding pairs — along with southern elephant seals hauled out on the same beaches. Most operators, including Lindblad, also report sightings of humpback whales and fin whales during the crossing.

The Antarctic Peninsula leg adds gentoo and chinstrap penguins, leopard seals, and occasional orca. The Falklands leg typically includes black-browed albatross, rockhopper penguins, and Magellanic penguins.

Why do landings get split into groups on some ships?

IAATO limits shore landings to 100 guests per site at any one time in Antarctica and South Georgia. Ships carrying more than 100 passengers — including Lindblad's 138–148-passenger vessels — must split guests into two rotating groups to stay compliant. While one group is ashore, the other waits aboard ship or on Zodiacs until it's their turn.

Smaller ships at or under the 100-guest threshold can land everyone simultaneously. Poseidon's 114-passenger Sea Spirit stays within that limit, which is the practical reason it's listed first in our alternatives comparison.
